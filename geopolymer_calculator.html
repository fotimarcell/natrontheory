The Kriven Formula

Precise geopolymer formulas are hard to find on the Internet. Not because it is high secret but because the field is still in development (=chaos). One of the leading scientists is Waltraud M. Kriven, we'll use her formula on this page for our calculations. This one:
M•AlO2•2*SiO2•5.5*H2O
Which looks absolutely crazy. Let me explain it in pure English.

Decyphering the formula

First of all, that capital M at the beginning of the formula is not an element you never heard of but a placeholder for either sodium (Na) or potassium (K). Both can be used as alkali activators.

Second, if we omit that bloody oxigen (O) we end up with a much more comprehensible version:
M•Al•2 x Si•5.5 x water
Now we can try decode the necessary parameters of our mixing.

What are the goals?

We have 3 goals, each of them deduced from the formula above:
  1. We need twice as much silicon atoms (Si) in the slurry than aluminum atoms (Al)
  2. We need to have 5.5 times more water than silicon (Si) atoms
  3. We need an alkaline (Na or K) pair to each and every Al atom

Or in short we need

1 part silicon, 2 part aluminum, 2 part "M", 5.5 part water

Molar masses

Molar mass it the weight of a single molecule. You can calculate it by reading the molar masses of individual atoms from the periodic table and adding them thogether.

Here is a simple example: H2O molar mass.
Hydrogen: 1. Oxigen: 16. H2O = 2 x 1 + 16 = 18
We'll use these molar masses later on:
  • Water (H2O) molar mass: 18
  • Waterglass (Na2SiO3) molar mass: 122
  • SiO2 molar mass: 60

Cat litter

To bring the waterglass solution's H2O : Si ratio to 5.5, we need to add some more silicon to the solution. But how? We cannot simply drop sand into the water and hope it will dissolve. It will not.

We need amorphous silicon as it is ready to dissolve in water. This is the part where cat litter comes into the picture. Yes. Those small white balls.

Cat litter is 100% amorphous SiO2, so we can simply add it to the waterglass solution in order to increase our silicon content.
