The Kriven Formula

Precise geopolymer formulas are hard to find on the Internet. Not because it was high secret but because the field is still in development (=chaos). One of the leading scientists is Waltraud M. Kriven, we'll use her formula on this page for our calculations. This:
M•AlO2•2*SiO2•5.5*H2O
Which looks absolutely crazy. Let me explain it in pure English.

Decyphering the formula

First of all, that capital M at the beginning of the formula is not an element you never heard of but a placeholder for either sodium (Na) or potassium (K). Both can be used as alcali activators.

Second, if we omit that bloody oxigen (O) we end up with a much more comprehensible version:
M•Al•2 x Si•5.5 x water
Now we can try decode the necessary parameters of our mixing.

What are the goals?

We have 3 goals, each of them deduced from the formula above:
  1. We need twice as much silicon atoms (Si) in the slurry than aluminum atoms (Al)
  2. We need to have 5.5 times more water than silicon (Si) atoms
  3. We need an alkaline (Na or K) pair to each and every Al atom

Or in short

1 silicon, 2 aluminum, 2 sodium (or potassium), 5.5 water

Molar masses

These are some constants we'll use throughout the calculations:
  • Waterglass (Na2SiO3): 122
  • Water (H2O): 18
  • SiO2: 60

Cat litter

In order to bring the waterglass solution's H2O : Na2SiO3 ratio to 5.5, we need to add some SiO2 to the solution. We'll need amorphous silicone as it is ready to dissolve in water.

This is the part where cat litter comes into the picture. Yes. Those small white balls.

Cat litter is 100% SiO2, so we can simply add it to the waterglass solution. We only need to calculate the correct amount of it.

Every ancient civilization with access to natron knew how to make waterglass. We see evidence of this everywhere (scoopmarks). And undoubtedly, they had access to wood ash. The question is whether they tried to mix the two. If they did, they could have created artificial stone. Let's recap what we know so far:
  • Molten natron etches quartz, consequently, granite
  • Etching granite produces waterglass
  • Waterglass is the main component of geopolymers
